## Why I Built This

I built GoldStar to explore whether short-term, verified incentives can meaningfully change student motivation.

---

Traditional academic rewards are delayed and abstract. This project experiments with a different assumption: that motivation improves when effort is acknowledged immediately and transparently, with community support instead of institutional funding.

GoldStar is intentionally scoped as an MVP to test this idea before expanding into more complex systems.

## Problem

Grades often feel meaningless to students.

Students study, take exams, and work hard â€” but the payoff is abstract and delayed. College, careers, and scholarships feel far away, while day-to-day needs and motivation are immediate.

As a result:
- Capable students disengage
- Academic effort is undervalued in the short term
- Motivation drops, especially for students who could succeed but donâ€™t see the point

At the same time:
- Parents and communities want to support students
- Schools want positive incentives without financial burden
- Existing reward systems are informal, delayed, or untrusted

**Core problem:**  
There is no trusted, scalable way to reward academic effort *in the moment*.

## How It Works (MVP)

1. Student creates an account
2. Student submits grades for a grading period
3. School verifies the submission
4. System assigns a reward tier
5. Student redeems a reward funded by sponsors

---

## Proposed Solution

GoldStar provides a platform where:
- Student grades are verified by schools
- Rewards unlock based on clear GPA tiers
- Funding comes from community sponsors
- Students redeem rewards immediately after verification

---

## Value Proposition

- **Students:** Academic effort finally feels worth it *now*
- **Schools:** Trusted validation without paying
- **Sponsors:** Visible, verified community impact

## Explicitly Out of Scope (MVP)

- Leaderboards or public rankings
- Gamification (streaks, badges beyond tiers)
- AI-based grade analysis
- Full SIS or district-level integrations

## Risks & Considerations

- Equity concerns (absolute performance vs improvement)
- Avoiding unhealthy competition
- Minimizing school workload
- Preventing fraud without complex systems
- Ensuring rewards support learning rather than distract from it

---

## Success Metrics (MVP Validation)

- Percentage of students who submit grades more than once
- Reward redemption rate
- Sponsor repeat contributions
- Verification turnaround time
- Fraud or dispute incidents (target: **zero**)
