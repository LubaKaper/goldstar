⭐ GoldStar

Tagline: Earn Gold for Your Grades

Overview

GoldStar is a student-facing platform that turns verified academic performance into immediate, tangible rewards funded by community sponsors.

Students earn rewards for their grades today — not years later.
Schools provide trusted verification.
Local businesses and donors invest directly in student success.

This document is a Product Requirements–style README focused on clarity and alignment, scoped tightly to an MVP.

⸻

Problem Statement

Grades often feel meaningless to students.

Students study, take exams, and work hard — but the payoff is abstract and delayed. College, careers, and scholarships feel far away, while day-to-day needs and motivation are immediate.

As a result:
	•	Capable students disengage
	•	Academic effort is undervalued in the short term
	•	Motivation drops, especially for students who could succeed but don’t see the point

At the same time:
	•	Parents and communities want to support students
	•	Schools want positive incentives without financial burden
	•	Existing reward systems are informal, delayed, or untrusted

Core problem:
There is no trusted, scalable way to reward academic effort in the moment.

⸻

Target Users

Primary Users
	•	Middle school & high school students (Grades 6–12)

Secondary Users
	•	Schools (administrators, counselors, or teachers as verifiers)
	•	Community sponsors (local businesses, donors)
	•	Parents / guardians (indirect stakeholders)

⸻

User Context & Constraints
	•	Students need motivation that feels fair, achievable, and real
	•	Schools require low administrative overhead
	•	Sponsors want verified impact, not self-reported claims

⸻

Product Goals & Success Criteria

Product Goals
	•	Increase student motivation through short-term, verified rewards
	•	Create a trusted loop: effort → verification → reward
	•	Enable community participation without school funding

Success Looks Like
	•	Students consistently submit grades
	•	Rewards are redeemed quickly after verification
	•	Sponsors return to fund additional reward pools
	•	No verified fraud or abuse during pilot

⸻

Current Alternatives & Gaps

How This Is Solved Today
	•	Parents offer informal rewards
	•	Schools provide recognition (honor roll, certificates)
	•	Scholarships reward long-term performance

Why These Fall Short
	•	Rewards are inconsistent and subjective
	•	Recognition is delayed and symbolic
	•	No transparent, scalable system exists

⸻

Proposed Solution (High-Level)

GoldStar provides a platform where:
	•	Student grades are verified by schools
	•	Rewards unlock based on clear GPA tiers
	•	Funding comes from community sponsors
	•	Students redeem rewards immediately

⸻

Value Proposition
	•	Students: Academic effort finally feels worth it now
	•	Schools: Trusted validation without paying
	•	Sponsors: Visible, verified community impact

⸻

MVP Scope & Functional Requirements

This MVP is designed to validate the core loop, not to scale.

Must-Have (P0)

Student Experience
	•	Create an account
	•	Submit grades for a grading period
	•	View verification status
	•	Unlock reward tiers after verification:
	•	Gold: 90–100
	•	Silver: 85–89
	•	Bronze: 75–84
	•	View and redeem rewards
	•	View basic reward history

School / Verifier Experience
	•	Review grade submissions
	•	Approve or reject submissions
	•	Verification action unlocks rewards

Sponsor Experience
	•	Create an account
	•	Contribute funds to a reward pool
	•	View high-level impact:
	•	Students rewarded
	•	Total funds distributed

⸻

Explicitly Out of Scope (MVP)
	•	Leaderboards or public rankings
	•	Gamification (streaks, badges beyond tiers)
	•	AI-based grade analysis
	•	Full SIS or district-level integrations
	•	Automatic grade syncing
(manual verification only for MVP)

⸻

Core User Flow (MVP)
	1.	Student signs up
	2.	Student submits grades for a term
	3.	School verifies grades
	4.	System assigns reward tier
	5.	Student redeems reward

⸻

Risks & Considerations
	•	Equity concerns (absolute performance vs improvement)
	•	Avoiding unhealthy competition
	•	Minimizing school workload
	•	Preventing fraud without complex systems
	•	Ensuring rewards support learning, not distract from it

⸻

Metrics for MVP Validation
	•	% of students who submit grades more than once
	•	Reward redemption rate
	•	Sponsor repeat contributions
	•	Verification turnaround time
	•	Fraud or dispute incidents (target: zero)

⸻

MVP Philosophy

GoldStar’s MVP exists to answer one question:

Do immediate, verified rewards meaningfully increase student motivation?

If the answer is yes, future iterations can expand into:
	•	Deeper school integrations
	•	Improvement-based rewards
	•	Long-term sponsorship programs
